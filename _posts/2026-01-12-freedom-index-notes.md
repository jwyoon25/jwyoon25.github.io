I skimmed the latest [**Economic Freedom of the World** (EFW) annual report](https://www.fraserinstitute.org/sites/default/files/2025-09/economic-freedom-of-the-world-2025-annual-report_1.pdf) (PDF)—the Fraser Institute’s country rankings with a year or two of lag on the data. It’s dry, but it’s a handy way to stop arguing in circles about “markets vs government” and actually look at **rules**: taxes and spending, courts and property rights, inflation, trade, and red tape.

The index doesn’t smuggle in “good outcomes.” It scores **how much room people have** to work, save, trade, and start stuff. You can still think some regulation is worth it; the report just measures the freedom side.

**The global headline:** scores climbed for ages, **peaked around 2019**, then dipped several years in a row. **Sound money** took the biggest hit—no surprise after the pandemic policy mix. Worth remembering next time someone assumes freedom only disappears in cartoon dictatorships; “temporary” stuff can leave a mark.

**South Korea** (where I’m from) usually lands in the **high 30s** out of ~165 countries—solid, not elite. That’s below the U.S. and way below Singapore-type poster children, but Korea is still rich and intense. The gap, to me, is less “unfree” than **messy domestic friction**: housing and land use, insider-heavy labour markets, services lagging manufacturing, plus **aging** that makes shrinking government on paper harder without real pension choices. We’re great at moving goods through Incheon; it’s the inside of the economy where rules often feel like a **tax on newcomers**.

**One stat that stuck with me:** in the freest countries, the **bottom tenth** doesn’t necessarily get a bigger *share* of national income—but they earn **way more in absolute terms** (purchasing power) than the bottom tenth in the least free places. So if you only stare at inequality **ratios**, you can miss whether poor people can actually **live**.

Last caveat: it’s **correlation**, not a lab experiment. Treat it as a diagnostic, not a cult leaderboard.

**Source:** [Economic Freedom of the World: 2025 Annual Report (PDF)](https://www.fraserinstitute.org/sites/default/files/2025-09/economic-freedom-of-the-world-2025-annual-report_1.pdf). More maps and tools at [efotw.org](https://www.efotw.org/).
